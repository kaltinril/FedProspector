what are all the types of identifiers for government contracts?

Here’s a structured breakdown of the main identifiers used in U.S. federal government contracting.

🏢 Entity Identifiers (Who You Are)
1. UEI (Unique Entity ID)

Issued in: SAM.gov

Replaced the old DUNS number (in 2022)

Required for all federal awards

12-character alphanumeric

Assigned when you register your entity in SAM

2. CAGE Code (Commercial and Government Entity Code)

Issued by: Defense Logistics Agency

5-character alphanumeric

Required for DoD contracts

Automatically assigned after SAM registration (for U.S. entities)

3. NCAGE Code

Same as CAGE but for non-U.S. companies

Issued through NATO systems before SAM registration

4. EIN / TIN

Issued by: Internal Revenue Service

Employer Identification Number

Used for tax reporting

Required to complete SAM registration

🏭 Business Classification Identifiers
5. NAICS Code

Issued by: U.S. Census Bureau

Defines your industry

Contracts are assigned a NAICS code

Determines small business size standards

6. PSC Code (Product Service Code)

Describes what the government is buying

Used for reporting and procurement classification

7. SIC Code

Older industry classification system

Mostly replaced by NAICS but still appears occasionally

🏷️ Small Business & Socioeconomic Certifications

These are not numeric IDs but status identifiers tied to your SAM profile:

Small Business (based on NAICS size standard)

8(a) Program – U.S. Small Business Administration

HUBZone

WOSB / EDWOSB

SDVOSB / VOSB

(Some programs require additional certification beyond SAM.)

📄 Contract & Award Identifiers
8. Contract Number / Procurement Instrument Identifier (PIID)

Unique number assigned to each contract

Example: W912DY-24-C-0001

9. Solicitation Number

Identifies a specific Request for Proposal (RFP) or Invitation for Bid

10. Task Order / Delivery Order Number

Used under IDIQ contracts or GWACs

🏛️ Agency & Location Identifiers
11. DODAAC (Department of Defense Activity Address Code)

Identifies DoD units and contracting offices

12. SAM Registration Status

Active / Inactive (must be Active to receive awards)

💳 Payment & Financial Identifiers
13. Bank Routing Number & Account Number

Used in SAM for ACH payments

14. TIN Matching / IRS Consent Status

Must validate against IRS records

🔎 Systems You’ll Encounter

SAM.gov – Entity registration
FPDS – Contract reporting
USASpending.gov – Public award data
beta.SAM.gov – Old portal name
